Website redesign brief

Laser Scanning

Redesign the website to improve organic enquiries, convert referrals more effectively and position the business as a complete engineering solutions provider rather than only a scanning company.

Primary objective

Make the business easier to understand, easier to trust and easier to contact.

Main business context

Most work comes from repeat customers and recommendations, but the site should improve organic enquiry generation.

Platform direction

Design in Figma. Build direction is Webflow, using the wireframe and references below to shape the final site.

Core problem

The current website does not make the full business obvious quickly enough

Users are not instantly seeing the full capability, the wider engineering support offering or the best next step. The site needs to validate trust quickly for referrals while also improving conversion for organic visitors.

Within 5 seconds, a visitor should understand that Laser Scanning can help capture, understand, validate and manufacture parts, and that it is easy to send a project over for review.
Business positioning

What the business is

Laser Scanning should be positioned as a one-stop engineering solutions provider.

It is not just a laser scanning company and it should not feel restricted to a small list of core services.

Important positioning rule

Lead with problems and outcomes

Do not lead the website with tools or equipment alone.

The site should first communicate the engineering problem being solved, then support that with the right methods, processes and capabilities.

Main capability groups

Structure the business into clear top-level service buckets

Data capture

  • Handheld laser scanning
  • Portable laser scanning
  • CMM laser scanning
  • Touch probe CMM
  • CT scanning

Reverse engineering

  • Simple to highly complex CAD work
  • Legacy parts
  • Design intent modelling
  • Replica methods where needed

Inspection & validation

  • GD&T work
  • High-accuracy inspection
  • Full ISIRs
  • Advanced reporting

Manufacturing & prototyping

  • 3D printing: FDM, SLA, SLS
  • CNC machining
  • Injection moulding
  • Zinc die casting
  • Vac forming and more

Engineering support

  • DFM
  • Supplier sourcing
  • Manufacturing advice
  • Supporting partners
  • End-to-end project assistance
Target user 1

Referral visitor

Already has some trust, but uses the site to validate credibility and check that Laser Scanning can handle their kind of job.

Target user 2

Organic search visitor

Has a specific engineering problem and is looking for the right route forward, not necessarily a specific tool or scanner.

Target user 3

Existing customer

Already trusts the business and simply needs a quick, low-friction way to send another enquiry or upload project files.

Homepage direction

Use the live wireframe as the main homepage structure

The live wireframe should be used as the primary basis for the homepage layout. The Elementor site should only be used as secondary inspiration for spacing, visual rhythm and general presentation.

Key homepage goals

  • Show breadth of capability without overwhelming the user
  • Build trust fast
  • Help users recognise their problem
  • Make contact feel easy

Key homepage sections

  • Hero
  • Trust strip
  • How we help
  • Core capability groups
  • Use cases
  • Case study
  • Final CTA

Key homepage message

  • We solve engineering problems
  • We support the whole journey
  • We work across many sectors
  • We are easy to engage with
Sector positioning

Do not over-claim sector specialism

Laser Scanning works across a very wide range of sectors and should be positioned as adaptable and outcome-focused.

The business should not pretend to be a deep specialist in every market. Instead, the message should be that the team works closely with each client to understand the engineering challenge and required result.

Automotive Infrastructure Manufacturing Energy Industrial equipment Specialist projects
Organic enquiry growth

Build pages around real problems

Organic growth should come from pages that match real user intent, not generic awareness content.

  • Reverse engineering without CAD
  • High-accuracy GD&T inspection
  • CT scanning for internal geometry
  • Large structure scanning
  • Recreating legacy parts
  • Prototype to production support
Design direction

What the design should feel like

  • Clean
  • Structured
  • Technical but clear
  • Confident, not flashy
  • Built around real visuals, not stock imagery
  • Consistent from page to page
UX principles

What every page needs to do

  • Make the next step obvious
  • Reduce friction to contact
  • Show credibility early
  • Help visitors identify their problem quickly
  • Balance breadth of service with clarity
Success criteria

How success should be judged

More website enquiries

Especially from organic search and referral traffic.

Lower bounce rate

Visitors should understand the offer faster and stay engaged longer.

Clearer positioning

The business should feel like a capable engineering partner, not just a scanning supplier.

Summary

Simple direction for the project

Use the live wireframe for structure, use the Elementor site for layout inspiration, and make the final site obvious, capable and easy to act on.
