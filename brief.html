Wireframe Brief
Project brief

Website redesign brief for Laser Scanning

The site needs to do a better job of positioning the business as a complete engineering support partner, improving organic enquiries and helping referred visitors feel confident enough to get in touch quickly.

Main commercial goal

Improve the quality and quantity of enquiries that come through the website.

Main messaging goal

Show that the business offers far more than standalone laser scanning.

Main UX goal

Make the site easier to understand, trust and act on.

Core issue

The site currently undersells the business

The main issue is not that the company lacks capability. It is that the current website does not communicate that capability clearly enough, quickly enough, or in a way that helps the right visitors identify themselves.

A visitor should not leave the site thinking “they do scanning.” They should leave thinking “they can probably solve my engineering problem.”
Business context

How the website fits the real business

Repeat work matters

Most business comes from existing customers, so the site must support credibility and make repeat contact easy.

Referrals matter

A lot of visitors will already have heard of the company, so the site needs to validate trust fast.

Organic growth still matters

The site should also attract better new enquiries from people actively searching for solutions.

Positioning

What the business should be positioned as

Not this

  • Just a laser scanning company
  • A narrow sector specialist
  • A tool-led technical supplier

More like this

  • A one-stop engineering support partner
  • A business that solves real project problems
  • A team that can capture, recreate, inspect and help manufacture parts
Lead with problems and outcomes. Use tools and processes as supporting proof, not as the main story.
Capability structure

Organise the offering into clear top-level groups

Data Capture

  • Handheld laser scanning
  • Portable laser scanning
  • CMM laser scanning
  • Touch probe CMM
  • CT scanning

Reverse Engineering

  • Simple to complex CAD
  • Legacy components
  • Design intent modelling
  • Replica methods

Inspection & Validation

  • GD&T
  • High-accuracy inspection
  • Full ISIRs
  • Dimensional reporting

Manufacturing & Prototyping

  • FDM, SLA, SLS
  • CNC machining
  • Injection moulding
  • Die casting / vac forming

Engineering Support

  • DFM
  • Supplier sourcing
  • Partner support
  • General engineering advice
Audience

Who the site has to work for

Referral traffic

Needs fast reassurance and proof that the business is credible and capable.

Search traffic

Needs clear answers to a specific engineering problem, often before caring which tools are used.

Existing customers

Need a fast, low-friction route to submit new jobs or start another conversation.

Homepage requirements

What the homepage must do well

Functional goals

  • Show breadth of capability clearly
  • Build trust quickly
  • Make contact easy
  • Guide users deeper into the site

Content goals

  • Present the business as problem-solving, not tool-selling
  • Use stronger use cases
  • Use better proof through case studies
  • Reduce generic engineering wording
Sector positioning

Show range without pretending to be experts in every market

The business works across many sectors. That is a strength, but it should be positioned carefully.

Recommended message

The company focuses on understanding the engineering challenge and adapting the solution to the client’s required outcome, regardless of sector.

Automotive Infrastructure Manufacturing Energy Industrial Equipment

What to avoid

  • Weak generic industry pages
  • Over-claiming expertise everywhere
  • Using sectors as the main site structure
Organic growth

Build pages around real engineering intent

Examples of strong page ideas

  • Reverse engineering without CAD
  • High-accuracy GD&T inspection
  • CT scanning for internal geometry
  • Large structure scanning
  • Recreating legacy parts
  • Prototype to production support

Why this is better

These page ideas align with serious buyer intent and are far more likely to bring relevant enquiries than broad awareness content.

Design direction

How the final site should feel

Visual direction

Clean, technical, structured, confident, not flashy.

Content direction

Clearer, less generic, more grounded in real work and believable use cases.

UX direction

Make the next step obvious and reduce friction at every stage.

Success measures

How success should be judged

Better enquiries

More relevant leads from both referrals and organic search.

Lower bounce rate

Visitors understand the offer faster and engage with more of the site.

Stronger positioning

The company feels like an engineering partner rather than just a supplier of scanning services.

Summary

Simple design direction

Use the wireframe for structure, the Elementor site for visual inspiration only, and make the final site clearer, more confident and more useful to real buyers.
This page should feel like a strategy document, not another homepage. Its job is to guide the design work and keep the project focused.
